ABOUT US
The Green Thumbs Up! Project or TGTUP is a Non-Governmental Organization (NGO) that aims to advocate kids' education for the less privileged children.
Imagine Passion
Imagine people from different walks of life, background, beliefs, profession and experiences all coming together for a shared purpose. Fueled by an imaginative heart that beats with thechildren, communities, and the environment, The Green Thumbs Up! Project (TGTUP) is now a reality as it engages in molding, motivating, and mobilizing kids for environmental leadership.
Persons
TGTUP goes beyond as an organization. It is a community building experience for the people taking part in the missions. The Mangyan community specifically the Iraya Tribe was the recent community that TGTUP visited. The community of volunteers keep growing in heart and number throughout the years. From a handful of people, the TGTUP now has close to a hundred volunteers from various parts of the country.

This network of volunteers is a pillar in the realization of the program’s objectives as they humbly serve as environmental ambassadors.
Partnerships
The TGTUP community thrives because of the people and organizations that continuously affirm their shared advocacy. Through the support of partners, TGTUP is enabled to share the message of environmental hope and educationto our communities. Some private and non-governmental organization, as well as some alumni and student organizations, are of the cherished partners of TGTUP. This community to community approach makes these partnerships sustainable and engaging.
Purpose
Caring for the environment and the love to share this to children and communities stands as the paragon objective of the project. The project features a combination of an environmental consciousness andimaginative thinking component for thekindergarten and elementary pupils, values formation and education as well as educational supplies distribution. On the part of the volunteers, every mission serves as an opportunity for building synergy in appreciating and reinforcing a community-basedconsciousness for the environment.
Possibilities
Harnessing the individual and collective contribution of the volunteers, partners, and communities, environmental leadership becomes a continuing realityin the next generation. Kids from kindergarten to elementary are taught the basics of environmental leadership. Through simple yet creative approaches like community and environment appreciation, tree planting sessions, imagination activities and even community singing, the ideals of environmental leadership are reinforced into the young minds.

As children are motivated and molded to become environmental champions in their own simple ways, this in effect mobilizes and challenges the community through their parents and families to also take an active role as good role models to their children.
